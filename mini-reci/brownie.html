Chocolate Chip Brownie Cookie Recipe

Chocolate chip cookie brownies, or "brookies," are a heavenly fusion of fudgy brownies and soft chocolate chip cookies,
offering the perfect blend of rich chocolate flavors and delightful textures in a single treat. These indulgent desserts are loved
for their contrasting layers of gooey brownie and chewy cookie dough, making them a favorite among dessert enthusiasts.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips (or milk chocolate chips if you prefer)
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (optional, such as walnuts or pecans)

How to make:

  1. Preheat the Oven:
    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Melt the Butter and Chocolate:
    • In a microwave-safe bowl or using a double boiler, melt the butter and 1 cup of semisweet chocolate chips until smooth. Stir occasionally to combine.
    • Once melted, remove from heat and allow it to cool slightly.
  3. Sift Dry Ingredients:
    • In a separate bowl, sift together the cocoa powder, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. This helps remove any lumps and ensures even mixing.
  4. Mix Wet Ingredients:
    • In a large mixing bowl, beat the eggs, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  5. Combine Wet and Dry Mixtures:
    • Gradually add the melted chocolate and butter mixture to the egg and sugar mixture. Stir until smooth and well combined.
  6. Fold in Chocolate Chips and Nuts (Optional):
    • Gently fold in 1 cup of semisweet chocolate chips (or milk chocolate chips) and chopped nuts, if using. This will create a rich and chunky cookie dough.
  7. Chill the Dough (Optional):
    • For thicker cookies, you can chill the dough in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes. Chilling the dough can help prevent the cookies from spreading too much during baking.
  8. Form Cookie Dough Balls:
    • Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving enough space between each cookie.
  9. Bake:
    • Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are set but the centers are still soft. Be careful not to overbake to keep the cookies fudgy.
  10. Cool:
    • Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  11. Enjoy:
    • Once cooled, your chocolate chip brownie cookies are ready to enjoy! Serve with a glass of milk for an extra treat.

These cookies are a delightful combination of brownie and chocolate chip cookie flavors. Enjoy them as a sweet snack or dessert.
